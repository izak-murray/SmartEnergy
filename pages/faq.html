Frequently Asked Questions
How is my energy usage calculated?
Estimated from your suburb, household size, and the appliances you selected at sign-up. Costs use current Origin Energy rates (30.8¢/kWh). Connect your smart meter in Settings for real-time data.
I'm a renter — can I still use Smart Energy?
Yes. All tips work without landlord approval — things like time-of-use shifting, appliance habits, and heating and cooling adjustments that require no physical changes to the property.
How do I split bills with flatmates?
Go to My Account and select "Add flatmates." The bill splitter divides costs based on individual usage patterns, so each person only pays for what they use.
How is my energy score calculated?
Your score is based on: usage vs similar households in your suburb (50%), tips you have completed (30%), and how much of your usage falls in off-peak periods (20%). Tap the score badge on your dashboard for a full breakdown.
Is my data private?
Your data is stored securely and never sold to third parties. Delete your account anytime from Settings to permanently remove all your data.
I have solar panels — how does Smart Energy use that?
When you sign up as a homeowner and enter your solar system size, we estimate your monthly generation, self-consumption, and feed-in tariff earnings. These appear on your dashboard. Connect your inverter via Settings for real-time accuracy.
How much could I save with solar?
A 3-6 kW system in Queensland typically generates 12-24 kWh per day, saving $50-$120 per month on your bill. You also earn a feed-in tariff (currently ~6.7-8¢/kWh with Origin Energy) for excess power sent to the grid.
What is an off-peak rate and when does it apply?
Off-peak electricity is cheaper because demand on the grid is lower. With Origin Energy in Queensland, off-peak periods are typically before 7am and after 10pm on weekdays, and all day on weekends.
How do I connect my smart meter?
Go to Settings and select "Connect smart meter." You will need your NMI (National Meter Identifier) from your electricity bill. Once connected, your dashboard will show real usage data instead of estimates.
Can I get solar as a renter?
Installing panels requires landlord consent, but you can ask your landlord to consider solar — it increases property value. Smart Energy's renter tips focus on shifting usage to off-peak periods for similar savings without any installation.
